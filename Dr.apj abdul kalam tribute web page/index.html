Dr.A.P.J.Abdul Kalam

The Man Who Great leader And Scientist In India

Here's a time line of Dr.A.P.J.Abdul Kalam life:

  • 1931 - Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931, to a Tamil Muslim family in the pilgrimage centre of Rameswaram on Pamban Island, Madras Presidency
  • 1960 - He joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) as a scientist
  • 1980 - In 1969, Kalam transferred to ISRO where he became the project director of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit in July 1980
  • 1998 - He also played a pivotal organisational, technical, and political role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998
  • 2002 - Kalam was elected as the president of India in 2002 with the support of both the ruling Bharatiya Janata Party and the then-opposition Indian National Congress
  • 2007 - In September 2007, he became the first chancellor of the newly established Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology at Thiruvananthapuram
  • 2011 - In 2011, Kalam voiced his support towards the establishment of the nuclear power plant at Koodankulam in Tamil Nadu, giving assurances for the safety of the facility
  • 2015 - 27 July 2015 (aged 83) Shillong, Meghalaya, India and his Resting place Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Memorial, Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India
  • Book - Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, known as the "Missile Man of India," authored several books, including "Wings of Fire: An Autobiography," "India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium," and "Ignited Minds: Unleashing the Power Within India"
